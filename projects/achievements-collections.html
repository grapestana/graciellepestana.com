The big concerns about thenew feature:
Why achievemets & collections for this audience?
• Our title today has no other objective than to play games indefinitely. The lack of metagames and long-term goals means that the Buraco Jogatina experience is based only on the intrinsic qualities of the product and on the cultural strength that the game itself possesses;

• Buraco players love to compete but as a traditional game they have concerns about big changes that impact the classical game mode;

• Achievements are challenges the player will receive and complete to acquire a Collectible as a reward, that can be used as customization for their account, not imposed as a barrier or main game content (Like quests and missions, for example);

• We believe that with the development of these two features, we will start the game's first metagame by building a completionist profile of players, assigning the long-term goal of acquiring all the game's content;

• These features will be the first step towards creating a conversion point, where free players will be encouraged to become premium to acquire the extra content of the feature.
How many competitors?
• We've a lot competitors but only two use systems with achievements and collections.
What makes us special?
• Our game is in the top 1 of both stores, so it's an improvement, especially for our users to continue being in the top 1 and to bring even more users;

• Buraco Jogatina is since 2006, and we have a lot of users that still in love with our app, so we have the duty to take care of and hear their concerns.

Benchmarks & Research




In parallel, we've worked on a survey in-app to research and understand better what users think about this type of feature, we've found some initial interests that helped a lot to start a Limited Time-Event. We've chosen LTE because through a survey it's difficult to show the idea to users that never played features like that, so even though we got a lot of good insights it's better to see users' interaction in production. But we've done a lot of tests even before launching the Limited Time-Event.







We've worked with our UX Research and Product Design Lead all this time. So, we chose to do the framework HEARTS.




Wireframes

After a long benchmark and research with our users we've started at the achievement flow, how users can see the available achievements, how is their feedback when achieved, how is the feedback to redeem a collectible, etc.



And all the flow of the collection, where users can see their collectibles, how they show as blocked when they still won't be achieved.







User Tests

We made a prototype to start user testing, we did 5 internal tests with coworkers already familiar with Buraco. After that we've started play test with our users that we've collected their contact in the last survey to contact them and invite them to play test the new feature.




After all these results, we've worked to improve the intuitiveness and the flow before start working on the user interface.
