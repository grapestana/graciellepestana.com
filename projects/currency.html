• Our principal competitors already have betting modes;

• After studies with marketing team we've rechead that ours users are more familiar to casino aesthetic;

Benchmarks

Wireframes

After researching our spades and casino competitors, we start to build wireframes for internal tests with the team.

Creating this flow helped us cover all sides of the process before testing it with our users.







Through the first wireframes, we had a clear idea of how to build the feature and started to work on bringing the New Lobby aesthetic to the currency system.







Also, we started working on Unity to bring the gameplay for user testing.







User Tests

To start testing the game, we did 5 internal tests with coworkers already familiar with spades. With the results in hand, we worked on Unity to implement the wireframes in-game and share them with users to test the complete experience.




The average age of our users is around 45 years old, not tech-savvy.

Despite the challenges of recruiting and creating tests for users in this age group, we succeeded in bringing in 6 users to try out the new features.







For this phase, I worked with a Product Owner and the Product Designer Led to find the best ways to achieve the right audience, and the results were better than we expected.







I built a Fake Door to understand user interest through the click rate of the new feature button. We tested with four words to see which was the most clicked. The word with the highest click was “Let's Bet”.

The results showed that users return an average of 2.5 times to the Fake Door. With this information, we can hypothesize that users want to see if there is anything new about the newest feature.







After that, we decided to launch our first Beta on the Android Store to see how it would perform. We reached 120 users through surveys and in-app notifications.

User Interface

For the final hi-fi prototype, I started by working with the elements of the new feature using grayscale swatches.







At this point, all the visuals are in the final Beta version of the feature, and we’re ready to launch the first release of the feature Currency System.




Documentations

Last but not least... Documenting all the processes used for the feature. That way, we have a case well-built for future research or updates.
